Latvia

Latvia is a country in the Baltic region of Northern Europe. It is bordered to the north by Estonia, to the south by Lithuania, to the east by Russia, and to the west by the Baltic Sea. The capital and largest city is Riga.

Geography

Latvia has a total area of 64,589 square kilometers (24,938 square miles). The terrain is mostly low and flat, with forests covering over 40% of the country. The highest point in Latvia is Gaizi≈Ükalns, which stands at 311.6 meters (1,022 feet) above sea level.

History

Latvia has a rich history that dates back to the 9th century. Throughout its history, Latvia has been occupied by various foreign powers, including Germany, Sweden, and the Soviet Union. Latvia declared its independence in 1918, but was occupied by the Soviet Union during World War II. Latvia regained its independence in 1991 following the collapse of the Soviet Union.

Culture

Latvian culture is a unique blend of traditional and modern influences. The country is known for its folk songs, dances, and festivals, which celebrate the country's rich cultural heritage. The Latvian cuisine is also distinct, with dishes such as grey peas and bacon, piragi (a type of pastry filled with bacon and onion), and sklandrausis (a sweet pastry filled with carrots and potatoes).

Tourism

Latvia is a popular tourist destination, known for its natural beauty and rich history. Some of the top tourist attractions in Latvia include:

  • Riga Old Town
  • Gauja National Park
  • Jurmala Beach
  • Cesis Castle
  • Kuldiga Old Town

Whether you're interested in history, culture, or outdoor activities, Latvia has something to offer.
